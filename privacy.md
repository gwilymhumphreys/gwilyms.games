# Privacy Policy — A Machine to Kill a God

Last updated: 2026-05-24

## What we collect

While the game is running, it sends anonymous gameplay events to our analytics provider:

- A random per-player ID generated on your machine (no name, email, or account identifier)
- Gameplay events: purchases, band clears, session start/end, frame rate, idle stretches
- Platform (Windows / macOS / Linux / Web), language, game version

## What we don't collect

- Your real name, email, or any account identifier
- Your IP address (our analytics provider may log it server-side; we never see or store it)
- Anything you type
- Save file contents

## Why

To understand where players get stuck, what balance changes work, and where the game needs improvement.

## Who else sees it

We use [Tokebi](https://tokebi-api.vercel.app) to receive and store the events. We don't share data with anyone else.

## Opting out

The title screen has a "Disable telemetry" button. After clicking it, no further events leave your machine. The setting persists across sessions.

## Deleting your data

Email gwilyms.games@gmail.com from any address — we'll remove all records tied to your player ID.
